Plantation

Plantations under the Tripura SCATFORM Project are not just about tree planting — they are a holistic tool for ecosystem regeneration, climate action, and inclusive development. By promoting community-based, ecologically sound, and economically beneficial plantation models, the project strengthens the foundation of Sustainable Forest Management (SFM) in Tripura.

Different types of Plantation Models:

  • Artificial Regeneration (AR) with mixed species: 1100 plants per hectare, in abandoned jhum sites where tree canopy density is less than 20% and where no teak stumps or seeds trees are available. The sites chosen must be within a distance of 5km from village. Need and choice of local villagers (JFMC) will be taken into account for deciding the species to be planted.
  • Aided Natural Regeneration (ANR) with mixed species: 200 plants per hectare, in lower catchment where tree canopy density is less than 20% and where teak stumps or seed trees or bamboo rhizomes are not available on the site. The site is chosen within a distance of 5 km village. Species has been planted as per the need and choice of local villagers (JFMC).
  • Teak Plantation Management: 200 plants per hectare, in existing degraded teak plantations, where tree canopy density is more than 20% and where teak stumps or seed are available on the site. The chosen site are within a distance of 5 km from village. Species has been planted as per the need and choice of local villagers (JFMC).

Project target

  • Silvi-pastoral plantation: 400 seedlings per hectare, near villages / settlements, where tree canopy density is less than 20%. The chosen sites are within a distance of 2-3 km from village. Species has been planted as per the fodder need and choice of local villagers (JFMC).
  • Filter strip & River bank Plantation: Filter strip will be developed on either side of streams/ chars, in flat area (up to 15% slope allowed), next to agricultural land/eroded areas. River bank Plantation will be carried out on eroded banks vulnerable to erosion on either side of the streams/chars. Existence of residential areas and agricultural land along the riverside is a pre-requisite for taking up riverbank plantation.
