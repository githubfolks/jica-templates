Vision & Mission

The SCATFORM Project represents a holistic and integrated approach to forest management in Tripura. It combines environmental conservation with socio-economic development to create a replicable model of sustainable forestry in India. The project's vision and mission reflect its commitment to building resilient ecosystems, empowering communities, and ensuring a sustainable future for Tripura's forests and people.

The vision of the Tripura SCATFORM Project is:

“To create ecologically stable and resilient forest ecosystems in the upper catchment areas of Tripura, while ensuring sustainable livelihoods, inclusive growth, and long-term environmental sustainability for forest-dependent communities.”

This vision is grounded in the following aspirations:

  • Ecological Sustainability: Rejuvenation of degraded forests, soil conservation, and water resource management in fragile catchment areas to maintain ecological balance.
  • Community Empowerment: Enabling tribal and rural communities to actively participate in forest management, decision-making, and income-generating activities.
  • Sustainable Livelihoods: Enhancing the quality of life of forest dwellers by promoting bamboo-based industries, non-timber forest products (NTFPs), eco-tourism, and skill development.
  • Climate Resilience: Building resilience against climate change through forest restoration, biodiversity conservation, and adaptive livelihood strategies.
  • Collaborative Governance: Creating a participatory governance model involving the Forest Department, Joint Forest Management Committees (JFMCs), Self Help Groups (SHGs), NGOs, and local institutions.

The mission of the SCATFORM Project can be outlined as follows:

“To improve forest quality and ecosystem services in the upper catchments of Tripura through sustainable forest management practices, integrated soil and moisture conservation measures, and active community participation, while promoting alternative livelihoods to reduce pressure on forests.”

The key mission components include:

  1. Forest and Ecosystem Restoration
    • Restoration of degraded forest land and enhancement of forest productivity.
    • Promotion of sustainable forestry practices, especially in areas affected by shifting cultivation.
    • Soil and moisture conservation measures to reduce erosion and improve water retention in catchment zones.
  2. Community Development and Livelihood Enhancement
    • Capacity building and skill development of JFMCs, SHGs, and forest-dependent communities.
    • Promotion of income-generating activities like bamboo cultivation and value-added processing of NTFPs (e.g., agarwood, broom grass, honey).
    • Support for eco-tourism and handicraft-based enterprises.
  3. Institutional Strengthening
    • Strengthening the institutional capacity of the Forest Department and related agencies to implement sustainable forest management.
    • Establishment and capacity building of decentralized forest governance bodies at village and community levels.
  4. Biodiversity Conservation
    • Protection of endemic and threatened species through conservation of natural habitats.
    • Awareness generation among communities about the value of biodiversity and sustainable resource use.
  5. Knowledge Management and Monitoring
    • Development of baseline surveys, GIS-based monitoring systems, and research to track forest health and project impact.
    • Knowledge dissemination through training, workshops, and community engagement.
