Sexual Harassment Committee

The Sexual Harassment Committee, formally known as the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) under the Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013, plays a crucial role in promoting a safe, dignified, and inclusive work environment. Since the Tripura JICA SCATFORM Project involves interactions among government staff, NGO workers, SHGs, and community members, the committee ensures that women are protected from sexual harassment during project implementation.

Roles and Responsibilities of the Sexual Harassment Committee

  • Create awareness about what constitutes sexual harassment and educate all stakeholders (staff, field workers, SHG members, community participants) about the rights of women under the Act.
  • Receive written complaints of sexual harassment from any aggrieved woman within the project ecosystem (employee, consultant, SHG member, community participant, etc.) and ensure the confidentiality of the complainant and the complaint.
  • Conduct fair, unbiased, and confidential inquiries into complaints received and follow the principles of natural justiceâ€”give both the complainant and respondent a chance to be heard.
  • Recommend appropriate disciplinary actions if allegations are proven (e.g., warning, suspension, termination) and If the complaint is found to be false with malicious intent, the ICC may recommend action against the complainant in accordance with the law.
  • Maintain detailed and confidential records of complaints, inquiry proceedings, evidence, and resolutions.
